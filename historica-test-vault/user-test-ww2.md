# World War II

The war in Europe began when Germany invaded Poland on September 1, 1939. Britain and France declared war on Germany two days later.

In April 1940, Germany invaded Denmark and Norway. The following month, the Wehrmacht launched its western offensive, sweeping through Belgium and the Netherlands. France fell in June 1940 after just six weeks of fighting.

The Battle of Britain raged from July to October 1940, as the Royal Air Force defended against the Luftwaffe bombing campaign. Germany launched Operation Barbarossa on June 22, 1941, invading the Soviet Union with over 3 million troops.

Japan attacked Pearl Harbor on December 7, 1941, bringing the United States into the war. The tide began to turn at the Battle of Stalingrad, which lasted from August 1942 to February 1943.

On June 6, 1944, Allied forces landed on the beaches of Normandy in the largest amphibious invasion in history. Paris was liberated on August 25, 1944.

Germany surrendered unconditionally on May 8, 1945. Japan surrendered on September 2, 1945, following the atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki.
